# Investor Q&A Coaching Templates

For every metric flagged **weak** or **critical**, generate three things in the report:

1. **The likely investor question** — phrased the way a VC would actually ask it.
2. **What NOT to say** — the defensive answer founders default to that hurts them.
3. **Recommended framing** — an honest, forward-looking response that acknowledges the weakness and shows a credible path.

If a metric is **strong**, do not coach — just acknowledge it briefly so the founder knows to lead with it.

## Runway < 12 months

- **Q:** *"How are you funding the next 18 months?"*
- **Don't:** blame the market or say "we're just about to close a round."
- **Do:** state current runway in months, name the specific cost cuts you've already made or will make by month X, and quantify the bridge or round size you're raising and against what milestone.

---

## Gross Margin below benchmark

- **Q:** *"Why is your gross margin only X%? How does that get to 70%+?"*
- **Don't:** claim it's "industry standard" if it isn't.
- **Do:** break out which line items (hosting, support, payment fees, services) drag margin, name the lever (e.g., move from AWS on-demand to reserved, automate tier-1 support), and commit to a target margin by a specific quarter.

## LTV:CAC < 3x

- **Q:** *"Your unit economics don't work yet — what changes that?"*
- **Don't:** argue the formula is wrong for your business.
- **Do:** show whether the gap is a CAC problem (too expensive to acquire) or an LTV problem (churn or ARPU), and point to the channel mix shift, pricing change, or retention initiative that closes it.

---

## Churn > 5% monthly

- **Q:** *"Why are customers leaving?"*
- **Don't:** attribute it to "wrong-fit customers" without evidence.
- **Do:** segment churn by cohort, plan, or acquisition channel; identify the segment driving it; describe the product or onboarding fix and the leading indicator you're tracking.

---

## Burn Multiple > 2x

- **Q:** *"You're burning $X to add $1 of ARR — how does that improve?"*
- **Don't:** promise efficiency "after the next hire."
- **Do:** name the specific spend categories you're trimming, the headcount plan, and the burn multiple target for the next two quarters.

---

## CAC Payback > 18 months

- **Q:** *"You can't afford to wait 18+ months to earn back CAC at this stage. Why so long?"*
- **Don't:** claim payback "doesn't apply" because of expansion revenue (unless you can prove NRR > 120%).
- **Do:** show the channel-by-channel payback, kill or shrink the worst channels, and name the pricing or annual-prepay lever that pulls payback forward.

---

## Revenue Growth < 5% MoM (early stage)

- **Q:** *"What's blocking faster growth?"*
- **Don't:** blame seasonality without data.
- **Do:** name the constraint (top-of-funnel, conversion, sales capacity), the experiment underway, and the leading indicator that would prove it's working.
